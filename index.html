Terms

FC = fleet commander

Logi = healers, they'll be remotely repairing your shields, Ospreys

DPS = damage per second, also what we call our high-damage ships, Caracals

Anchor = player to orbit closely

Yellow-box = rats have target-locked you

Red-box = rats are shooting at you

Prop mod = propulsion module, afterburner/microwarpdrive

AB = afterburner

Primary = current target to shoot

Secondary = next target to shoot

SRP = ship replacement program

Fleet Operation

Make sure safeties are kept green!

Acceleration Gates

Do NOT take gates unless FC has given the go-ahead. (They'll say something like "Gate is green" or "Fleet take gate.") If you're unsure, assume gates are red and ask in fleet chat.

If FC asks you to align down the gate, double-click in space in the direction the gate is pointing, or use 'q' + double-click. Keep prop mods off. Be careful to not overshoot gates when aligning (you have to be within 2.5km to activate a gate), and make sure you're not bumping into the gate.

Anchor

Once inside a room, Caracals/DPS should anchor on the DPS anchor, and Ospreys should anchor on the Logi anchor. To anchor, orbit your designated player at 2500 meters (instead of keep at range). The goal is to maintain transversal to mitigate damage from rats. Your anchor will then move you around.

Keep prop mods off while orbiting. If you start falling behind, or your anchor tells you to, turn on your prop mod for a few cycles.

Be aware of your surroundings and make sure you are not bumping into structures. You will take significantly more damage if you get stuck in one place.

Broadcasts

Make sure to always have the Fleet History window open (Neocom ‚Üí Social ‚Üí Fleet ‚Üí History tab).

Broadcast Need Shield immediately on yellow-box. Once the rats go yellow ‚Üí red ‚Üí yellow-box, broadcast In Position At.

If you fall under 30% capacitor, broadcast Need Capacitor. Once you've obtained at least 70% capacitor, broadcast In Position At. Keep in mind logi will not always be able to give out capacitor.

Tags

Instead of broadcasting targets, FC will tag rats in the order in which to shoot them. Tag 1 means shoot first, higher tags mean shoot later. Prioritize frigates first since they tend to scram/web.

Make sure you have the tags column enabled in your overview. Go to Overview Settings ‚Üí Columns ‚Üí check the Tag box.

Watchlist

DPS ships should keep FC and anchor on watchlist. Logi should keep as many players as possible on watchlist, especially newer players.

You can anchor from watchlist. Right-click and select orbit at 2500 meters.

To add someone to watchlist, right-click their name ‚Üí Fleet ‚Üí Add to Watch List.

Drones

Don't forget to launch your drones and have them engage your target. Recall drones if FC says to "pull drones."

If your drones start taking damage, recall them immediately. If you think a drone won't make it back in time, broadcast it as a target and logi will try to help save it. However, logi will prioritize ships over drones.

Emergencies

If for some reason the incoming dps is too much for logi to handle and your shield starts getting very low, overheat your hardeners and start warping out if you're not scrammed. Make sure your afterburner/microwarpdrive is off when initiating warp. Do not worry about leaving drones behind.

If you accidentally lose connection and/or drop fleet, notify us as soon as possible (use Discord if necessary).

Logistics

Comms

You can chat over voice comms during missions, but if FC/anchors interrupt you, please stay silent until they've finished talking. They might say "comms," "clear comms," or "check check" to get the fleet's attention.

Between Missions

Use the time between missions for bio breaks and looting wrecks.

Ospreys should refit shield hardeners for the next mission if needed. Caracals should load the correct missile type for the next mission.

Loot + Salvage

Loot and salvage is first-come first-serve, but ask FC before dropping any MTUs (only one MTU per room to prevent them from fighting over wrecks). Bookmark sites that you want to come back to. FC will also try to bookmark each site in a shared folder.

You can keep the loot you pickup, or donate it to the corp to fund T2 fittings. Ask FC about how to donate.

No one should be looting/salvaging while a mission is still being run. You can use the time between missions or after fleet to do that.

Rewards

Bounties, mission rewards, standing gains, and loyalty points will automatically be split evenly among the fleet.

SRP

If you lose a ship due to FC/anchor/logi error, we can help you get it replaced. We will cover everything you've lost that insurance does not cover. To participate in the program, send 1 million ISK to your FC when you join fleet, with the description "SRP". You will be covered while you are in fleet, up until downtime.

We will not be able to cover your loss if you broadcasted for shield late, failed to get to your anchor, took a gate early, brought the wrong fit, or failed to follow instructions in any other way.

Why use cruisers for L4 missions?

Most people run L4 missions on their own in a battleship or equivalent, so why would we use tech 1 cruisers?

The point of these PvE fleets is to familiarize newer players with fleet operations and commands, such as watchlist, broadcasts, and anchoring. This is required knowledge for PvP fleets. We also run our fleets nearly exactly the same way WTM runs their incursion fleets, so this could be a way to practice flying in a PvE fleet without risking a 1 billion ISK ship.

Furthermore, it can often be hard for newbros to get involved in more advanced PvE activities, since they don't yet have the necessary skills and/or ships. We hope these fleets give them a chance to actively participate in L4 missions when they would otherwise be on salvage duty.

Doing things in groups is also more fun! EVE is not meant to be played solo.
